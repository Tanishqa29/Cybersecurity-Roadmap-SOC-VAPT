## 🔹 Transmission Media

Transmission media is the path through which data travels from sender to receiver. It can be wired (guided) or wireless (unguided).

---

## 🔸 Types of Transmission Media

### ▪️ 1. Guided Media (Wired)

#### 🔹 Twisted Pair Cable
It consists of two insulated copper wires twisted together to reduce interference. It is commonly used in LAN and telephone lines.

**Example:** Ethernet cables  

---

#### 🔹 Coaxial Cable
It has a central conductor with insulation and outer shielding for better signal quality. It provides higher bandwidth than twisted pair.

**Example:** Cable TV connection  

---

#### 🔹 Fiber Optic Cable
It transmits data using light signals through glass or plastic fibers. It offers very high speed, long-distance communication, and high security.

**Example:** JioFiber / Airtel Fiber  

---

### ▪️ 2. Unguided Media (Wireless)

#### 🔹 Radio Waves
Used for wireless communication over long distances. Signals can travel through walls and obstacles.

**Example:** WiFi, radio broadcasting  

---

#### 🔹 Microwaves
Used for point-to-point communication and requires line-of-sight. It supports high data transmission rates.

**Example:** Satellite communication  

---

#### 🔹 Infrared
Used for short-range communication and cannot pass through walls.

**Example:** TV remote control  

---

## 🔥 Key Difference

| Feature        | Guided Media        | Unguided Media      |
|----------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Medium         | Physical cables     | Air (wireless)      |
| Speed          | High (especially fiber) | Medium to high     |
| Security       | More secure         | Less secure         |
| Cost           | Higher installation | Lower setup cost    |

---

## 🔹 Interview Question
**Q: What is transmission media?**  
**A:** It is the medium through which data is transmitted from one device to another, either through cables or wirelessly.
