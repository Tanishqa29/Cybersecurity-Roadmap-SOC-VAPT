## 🔹 Switching

Switching is the process of forwarding data from source to destination through intermediate devices in a network. It helps reduce the number of direct connections between devices and improves efficiency.

---

## 🔹 Why Switching is Needed
In a network, connecting every device using point-to-point links is complex and costly.  
Switching uses a central device (like a switch) to connect all devices, similar to a star topology.

---

## 🔹 Types of Switching

### ▪️ 1. Circuit Switching
A dedicated communication path is established between sender and receiver before data transfer. The path remains reserved during the entire session.

**Example:** Traditional telephone calls  

---

### ▪️ 2. Packet Switching
Data is divided into small packets and sent independently through different paths. Packets are reassembled at the destination.

**Example:** Internet  

---

### ▪️ 3. Message Switching
The entire message is sent as a single unit and stored temporarily at intermediate devices before forwarding.

**Note:** It is slower and mostly outdated  

---

## 🔥 Key Points
- Reduces network complexity  
- Improves data transmission efficiency  
- Used in modern communication systems  

---

## 🔹 Interview Question
**Q: What is switching?**  
**A:** Switching is the process of forwarding data from one device to another through a network using different switching techniques.
