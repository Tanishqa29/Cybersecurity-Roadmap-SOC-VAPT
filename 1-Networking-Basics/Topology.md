## 🔹 What is Topology?
Topology in networking means the way devices (computers, servers, routers, etc.) are arranged and connected in a network.

👉 It describes how a network is designed and how devices communicate.

---

## 🔹 Types of Network Topology

### 1. Bus Topology
All devices are connected to a single communication line (bus) which carries messages in both directions.

**Pros:**
- Simple and cheap  

**Cons:**
- If main cable fails → entire network goes down  
- Performance decreases with more devices  

---

### 2. Star Topology ⭐ (Most Common)
All devices are connected to a central hub or switch. Devices do not communicate directly; data is sent to the hub, which forwards it to the destination.

**Pros:**
- Easy to manage  
- Failure of one device does not affect others  

**Cons:**
- If hub/switch fails → whole network goes down  

---

### 3. Ring Topology
Devices are connected in a circular loop. Data travels in one direction from one device to another.

**Pros:**
- No data collision  

**Cons:**
- Failure of one device or link affects entire network  

---

### 4. Mesh Topology 🔗
Every device is connected to every other device in the network.

**Pros:**
- Highly reliable  
- No single point of failure  

**Cons:**
- Expensive  
- Complex to set up  

---

## 🔹 Interview Question
**Q: What is network topology?**  
**A:** It is the arrangement of devices in a network and how they are connected and communicate with each other.
