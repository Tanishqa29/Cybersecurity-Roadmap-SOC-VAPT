# Episode 02 - Identity and Access Management (IAM)

## Overview

Identity and Access Management (IAM) ensures that only authorized users can access enterprise resources.

---

## Core Principles

- Authentication
- Authorization
- Accounting

---

## Authentication Methods

- Password
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Biometrics
- Smart Cards
- Single Sign-On (SSO)

---

## Authorization Models

- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC)

---

## Principle of Least Privilege

Users should receive only the permissions necessary to perform their work.

---

## Enterprise Example

Finance employees should not have access to HR databases.

Developers should not access production servers without approval.

---

## Best Practices

- Enable MFA
- Disable inactive accounts
- Review permissions regularly
- Use strong password policies
- Implement Privileged Access Management (PAM)

---

## Key Takeaways

IAM protects enterprise resources by ensuring the right people have the right access at the right time.
